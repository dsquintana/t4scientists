Chapter 5 Common twitter acronyms and abbreviations

This will change over time, but here’s a list of acronyms that you might see on twitter, and could find handy to use yourself. Of course, there’s a huge list of acronyms and abbreviations associated with texting and the internet, so I’m only going to cover ones that generally Twitter-specifc.

  • ICYMI: In case you missed it. Often used as a reply to a tweet, in order to re-introduce a tweet for a different timezone. You can also quote retween your own tweet, writing “ICYMI”
  • HT: Hat tip. Used as a way of acknoledging the source of infomation, typically in the real work but also electronically. For instance, you may have discovered something talking to a collegue at work, which you then want to share on Twitter. If your collegue is on twitter, you can acknoledge them by adding “HT (???)” at the end of the tweet. This is occasionally written as H/T as well -RT: Retweet. Before retweeting was a formal feature, people used to copy and paste other people’s tweets, along with their username, and begin these tweets by “RT”. This abreviation is still used today
  • TIL: Today I learned. Used to explain something new that you learnt, which is typically surprising. (e.g, “TIL you can SMS your tweets”)
